Frequently Asked

Everything you might
want to know first.

It's completely normal to have questions before reaching out. These are the ones Amna hears most often — answered honestly.

Do I have to be religious or practicing to come here?

+
Not at all. mymuslimtherapists works with Muslims across the full spectrum — from deeply practicing to questioning to culturally Muslim but not religiously active. The space is informed by cultural understanding and Islamic values, but it is not a requirement that clients practice, believe, or identify with Islam in any particular way. You are welcome exactly as you are.

Will Amna bring religion or Islam into our sessions?

+
Only if you want her to. Faith-integrated therapy is offered and meaningful for many clients — but it's always optional and client-directed. Amna will never impose religious framing, quote Quran or hadith without invitation, or assume that faith is relevant to your work. If it's meaningful to you, she'll weave it in. If not, she won't. You set the terms.

What if a family member finds out I'm in therapy?

+
Your privacy is protected under federal law (HIPAA) and Georgia state ethics guidelines. Amna cannot and will not share your participation in therapy with family members, spouses, parents, employers, imams, or anyone else without your explicit written consent. The only exceptions are narrow, legally mandated situations involving immediate safety — and Amna will always explain these limits clearly in your first session.

How long does therapy usually take?

+
It varies significantly by person and goal. Some clients resolve a specific issue in 8–12 sessions. Others find ongoing therapy valuable for a year or more. There's no fixed timeline — and no pressure. In your first session, you and Amna will discuss your goals and what a reasonable timeframe might look like. You can always reassess together as you go. You're never locked in.

What should I expect from my very first session?

+
Your first session is a thorough but relaxed conversation — not a test, not an interrogation, and not something you need to prepare for. Amna will ask about your background, your life, and what's been weighing on you. You share as much or as little as you're comfortable with. The goal of the first session is simply to get to know each other and build a foundation of trust. There are no wrong answers and no judgment.

Is online therapy really as effective?

+
Yes — for most concerns, research consistently shows that telehealth therapy produces outcomes equivalent to in-person sessions. This includes anxiety, depression, relationship issues, life transitions, grief, and many other common presentations. Amna uses a HIPAA-compliant video platform that is secure and stable. The only potential limitation is for certain types of advanced trauma processing work — Amna will be transparent with you if she ever feels in-person would genuinely serve you better.

What if Amna and I aren't a good fit?

+
That's exactly what the free consultation is for — to make sure there's a genuine fit before any commitment. If at any point during the therapeutic process you feel it's not working, Amna will always support you in finding a better match. The therapeutic relationship only works when trust is real, and Amna would rather help you find the right therapist than keep working together out of obligation.

Can I bring my spouse or a family member to sessions?

+
It depends on the type of therapy. Couples sessions, family therapy, and some adolescent therapy involve other people by design. For individual therapy, sessions are one-on-one unless you and Amna discuss and agree to have someone join for a specific session with a clear purpose. Your individual sessions are your space — there's no obligation to include anyone else.
