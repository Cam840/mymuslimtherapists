My Story

A therapist who understands
your world.

Amna Ahmad is a Licensed Professional Counselor with over a decade of clinical experience working with Muslim individuals, couples, and families. Born and raised in a Muslim household herself, Amna understands firsthand the unique intersection of faith, culture, family expectation, and personal identity that her clients navigate every day.

She founded mymuslimtherapists after years of watching Muslim clients spend their precious therapy time explaining cultural context — their family dynamics, their faith practices, why they couldn't "just leave" or "just talk to an imam." She knew there had to be a better way. A space where clients could walk in and simply be known.

"I wanted to create a place where you never have to translate yourself. Where your faith, your family, and your cultural identity are not obstacles to understand — they're the starting point."

A Clinical Foundation in Evidence-Based Care

Amna's approach is grounded in established, research-supported therapeutic frameworks. She draws from Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), trauma-informed care, and family systems theory — adapting each modality to fit the individual in front of her rather than applying a one-size-fits-all model.

For clients who wish to incorporate faith, Amna is fluent in Islamic concepts of sabr (patience), tawakkul (trust in God), muhasaba (self-reflection), and shukr (gratitude) — and knows how to weave them alongside clinical frameworks in ways that feel authentic, not performative. For clients who prefer a purely secular approach, that is equally honored.

Who She Works With

Amna specializes in supporting Muslim adults, teens, couples, and families navigating anxiety, depression, trauma, relationship challenges, parenting, life transitions, and identity questions. She has a particular passion for second-generation Muslim Americans who feel caught between cultures, women carrying invisible burdens in silence, and individuals processing religious or intergenerational trauma.

She works with Muslims across the full spectrum of practice — from the devout to the questioning to the non-practicing — and believes every person deserves compassionate care without judgment about where they fall on that spectrum.

Beyond the Office

When she's not in session, Amna is an avid reader of Islamic philosophy and contemporary psychology, a passionate cook who believes food is a form of cultural memory, and a mother who knows firsthand the juggling act of faith, family, and self-care. These human experiences make her not just a better therapist — but a more present one.

Therapeutic Philosophy

How Amna approaches healing.

Therapy with Amna is collaborative, honest, and rooted in both clinical science and cultural sensitivity. Here are the core beliefs that guide every session.

01

You are the expert on your own life.

Amna's job is not to tell you what to do — it's to help you understand yourself more deeply, so you can make choices that are truly yours. She will offer tools, frameworks, and perspective, but you remain the author of your own story throughout the therapeutic process.

02

Culture is not a complication — it's context.

Traditional therapy often treats cultural and religious factors as obstacles to "Western" psychological models. Amna sees it differently: your culture, your faith, and your family dynamics are not complications to work around. They are the essential context in which your healing happens.

03

Evidence matters — and so does your intuition.

Amna uses research-backed methods because they work. She also deeply respects each client's inner wisdom, lived experience, and sense of what feels right. The best therapy happens when science and self-knowledge work together, not when one overrides the other.

04

Progress, not perfection.

Healing is rarely linear. There will be hard sessions and easier ones, breakthroughs and setbacks. Amna holds space for all of it — without rushing, without judgment, without an agenda for how your healing is supposed to look. The only goal is meaningful, sustainable change on your terms.

05

The therapeutic relationship is the foundation.

Research consistently shows that the quality of the therapist-client relationship is one of the strongest predictors of therapy outcomes. Amna invests in genuine connection, real trust, and deep listening — because everything else builds from there.

06

You don't need to be in crisis to deserve support.

Many Muslim clients wait until they're overwhelmed before seeking help — because asking for support can feel like admitting weakness. Amna rejects that framing entirely. Proactive care, maintenance, and growth are all equally valid reasons to be in therapy. You don't have to be broken to deserve to feel better.

Core Values

What this practice stands for.

1

Cultural Safety

Every client deserves a space where their cultural identity — including their faith — is treated as an asset, not a barrier. We actively work to ensure Muslim clients feel seen, safe, and never othered in their own therapy sessions.

2

Radical Confidentiality

In communities where stigma around mental health is real, we take privacy seriously. Your sessions, your records, and your presence here belong to you alone. We maintain the highest standards of ethical confidentiality at all times.

3

Non-Judgment

Whether you're struggling with your faith, your marriage, your mental health, or something you've never said out loud — there is no judgment here. This practice exists precisely for the things that are hardest to say anywhere else.

4

Clinical Excellence

Being a Muslim-centered practice does not mean compromising on clinical standards. We use evidence-based methods because they produce real results — and our clients deserve nothing less than excellent care.

5

Accessibility

Mental health care should not be a privilege of the few. We offer telehealth throughout Georgia, work to accommodate schedules, and are committed to discussing financial accessibility with clients who need it.
