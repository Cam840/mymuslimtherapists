Meet Your Therapist

A space where you never have to
explain yourself.

Amna Ahmad is a Licensed Professional Counselor with over a decade of clinical experience working with Muslim individuals, couples, and families. She founded mymuslimtherapists with a singular purpose: to create a therapeutic space where Muslim clients never have to translate their faith, justify their cultural values, or set aside their identity in order to receive excellent mental health care.

Her approach is integrative and evidence-based — drawing from Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), trauma-informed care, and family systems frameworks — while remaining deeply attentive to each client's relationship with their faith. For some clients, that means weaving Islamic concepts like sabr, tawakkul, and shukr into the therapeutic conversation. For others, it simply means working with a counselor who understands their world without needing it explained. Both are equally welcome here.

Amna has worked across university counseling centers, community mental health settings, and private practice. She is particularly passionate about supporting second-generation Muslim Americans, women navigating multiple roles, and those carrying the weight of intergenerational or religious trauma. She believes deeply that healing is possible — and that no one should have to face it alone.

My approach: warm, collaborative, and evidence-rooted.

Therapy with Amna is a partnership. Together, you work to understand what's happening, why it matters to you, and what meaningful change might look like in your life. She leans on what the research shows about effective treatment, while trusting that you are the expert on your own life, your faith, and what feels right for you.

Our Philosophy

Therapy that honors who you are.

Grounded in evidence, informed by culture, and open to faith — our approach is built around you as a whole person, not just a presenting concern.

Faith-Integrated Care

For clients who want it, we weave Islamic concepts of patience, gratitude, trust, and self-reflection alongside modern therapeutic methods. For those who don't, your beliefs are respected and never questioned. The choice is always yours.

Evidence-Based Methods

We rely on therapeutic approaches with strong research support — CBT, ACT, trauma-focused care, emotion-focused therapy for couples, and family systems work — all adapted to your cultural and personal context.

Whole-Person Healing

You're more than a diagnosis. We consider your relationships, community, spiritual life, work, and history — because real healing happens when all parts of you are acknowledged and supported together.

Cultural Humility

We don't assume — we listen. Every Muslim client comes from a unique cultural, ethnic, and family background. Your therapist's role is to understand your specific world, not apply generalizations about what Muslim life looks like.

Privacy & Discretion

We understand the cultural weight of seeking mental health support. Your sessions, your records, and your participation here are held in the strictest legal and ethical confidence — always.

No Judgment, Ever

This is a space to be honest about where you actually are — not where you think you should be. There is no version of yourself that needs to be hidden, fixed, or apologized for when you walk through our door.

Frequently Asked

Common questions,
honest answers.

Starting therapy brings up a lot of questions. Here are the ones we're asked most often by new clients — answered straightforwardly.

Do I have to be religious to come here?

Not at all. We work with Muslims across the full spectrum of belief and practice — from devout to questioning to non-practicing. Our space is shaped by cultural understanding, not religious requirement. Whatever your relationship with faith looks like, you are welcome here exactly as you are.

Will my therapist bring religion into sessions?

Only if you want them to. Faith-integrated therapy is available and meaningful for many clients — but it's always offered, never imposed. Many clients simply want a therapist who understands their cultural context, and that alone is more than enough.

How private is therapy? My family is close-knit.

Therapy is legally and ethically confidential. We never share information with family members, employers, or anyone else without your written consent — with only very narrow, legally mandated exceptions related to immediate safety. Your sessions are yours alone.

How long does therapy typically take?

It depends entirely on your goals. Some clients address a specific concern in 8–12 sessions; others find ongoing therapy valuable over many months or longer. We'll discuss your goals in your first session and revisit them regularly. The pace is always guided by your progress and your preferences.

Is online therapy really as effective?

For most concerns — anxiety, depression, relationships, life transitions — research consistently shows telehealth is as effective as in-person therapy. Some highly specialized work may be better suited to an in-person setting. We'll discuss which option is the best fit for your situation during the consultation.

How do I know if Amna is the right therapist for me?

That's exactly what the free 15-minute consultation is for. You'll get a real sense of Amna's approach and personality, and she'll make sure she can genuinely help with what you're going through. If she's not the right fit, she'll do her best to connect you with someone who is.
