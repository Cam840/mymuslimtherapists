Meet Your Therapist

A space where you don't have to explain yourself.

Dr. Fatima Hassan is a licensed psychologist with over 15 years of clinical experience supporting Muslim individuals, couples, and families through anxiety, depression, trauma, marital challenges, parenting struggles, identity questions, and life transitions. She founded mymuslimtherapists with a clear vision: to create a clinical space where Muslim clients never have to translate their faith, justify their values, or set aside their identity to receive excellent mental health care.

Her approach is integrative — drawing from Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), trauma-focused care (EMDR-trained), and family systems work — while remaining deeply respectful of each client's relationship with their faith. For some clients, that means weaving Islamic concepts like sabr, tawakkul, and shukr into the therapeutic conversation. For others, it simply means knowing that their therapist understands their cultural context without explanation. Both are equally welcome here.

Dr. Hassan has worked across community mental health, university counseling centers, and private practice. She is particularly passionate about the mental health needs of second-generation Muslim Americans, women navigating multiple roles, and those carrying the invisible weight of intergenerational and religious trauma.

My approach in three words: warm, honest, evidence-based.

Therapy with me is collaborative. We work together to understand what's happening, why it matters, and what change might look like for you. I lean on what the research tells us about effective treatment — and I trust that you are the expert on your own life, your faith, and what feels right for you.

Our Philosophy

Therapy that honors who you are.

We blend evidence-based clinical care with deep cultural humility — so you can focus on healing instead of explaining.

01

Faith-Integrated Care

For clients who want it, we incorporate Islamic concepts of patience, gratitude, trust, and self-reflection alongside modern therapeutic methods. For those who don't, your beliefs are still respected and never questioned. The choice is always yours.

02

Evidence-Based Methods

We rely on therapeutic approaches with strong research support — CBT, ACT, EMDR for trauma, emotion-focused therapy for couples, and family systems work — adapted to your cultural and personal context.

03

Whole-Person Healing

You're not just a diagnosis. We consider your relationships, your community, your spiritual life, your work, and your past — because real healing happens when all of these are seen and supported together.

04

Cultural Humility

We don't assume — we listen. Every Muslim client comes from a unique cultural, ethnic, and family background. Your therapist's job is to understand yours, not to apply generalizations.

05

Privacy & Discretion

We understand the cultural weight of seeking mental health support. Your sessions, your records, and your participation here are held in the strictest confidence — protected legally and ethically.

06

No Judgment, Ever

This is a space to be honest about where you actually are — not where you think you should be. There is no version of you that needs to be hidden, fixed, or apologized for here.

Frequently Asked

Common questions, honest answers.

Do I have to be religious to come here?

Not at all. We work with Muslims across the full spectrum of practice and belief — from devout to questioning to non-practicing. Whatever your relationship with faith looks like, you'll be respected. Our work is shaped by cultural understanding, not religious requirement.

Will my therapist try to bring religion into sessions?

Only if you want them to. Faith-integrated therapy is available on request, but it's never imposed. Many of our clients want a therapist who simply understands their cultural context — and that's enough on its own.

How private is therapy? My family is close-knit.

Therapy is legally and ethically confidential. We do not share information with family members, employers, or anyone else without your written consent — with very narrow exceptions related to immediate safety. Your sessions are yours alone.

How long does therapy take?

It depends on your goals. Some clients work through a specific issue in 8–12 sessions; others find ongoing therapy supportive over many months or years. We'll discuss your goals and timeline during your first session and revisit them regularly.

What if I can't afford therapy?

We accept most insurance plans, which significantly reduces out-of-pocket costs. We also offer a limited number of sliding-scale spots based on financial need — just ask. Mental health care should not be a luxury.

Is online therapy as effective as in-person?

For most concerns — anxiety, depression, life transitions, relationship issues — research shows online therapy is just as effective as in-person care. Some specialized work, like certain trauma processing, may be better in person. We'll discuss what's right for you.
