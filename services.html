Service 01

Individual
Therapy

One-on-one sessions with Amna, tailored entirely to your needs, goals, and pace. Individual therapy is a space to talk openly about what's weighing on you — without editing yourself for family, community, or cultural expectations. Whether you're navigating anxiety, processing a painful experience, struggling with your sense of self, or simply feeling stuck, individual therapy creates the conditions for real, lasting change.

Amna uses an integrative approach that combines Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, Acceptance and Commitment Therapy, and other evidence-based tools — always adapted to your cultural background and your relationship with your faith.

What you'll work on:

Understanding the root causes of your distress — not just managing symptoms. Building lasting coping skills. Processing difficult memories or experiences. Improving your relationship with yourself, your faith, and your emotions. Creating a life that feels more aligned and less exhausting.

Ideal for: Adults experiencing anxiety, depression, burnout, life transitions, identity questions, self-worth struggles, relationship stress, or anyone simply ready to invest in their mental wellbeing.

Service 02

Couples &
Marriage Counseling

Couples therapy is for any partnership that wants to communicate better, understand each other more deeply, and grow stronger through difficulty — not just survive it. Amna offers both pre-marital counseling for couples preparing for marriage and ongoing couples work for those navigating conflict, disconnection, or major life transitions together.

Her approach is informed by emotion-focused therapy and Gottman Method principles, thoughtfully adapted to honor Islamic values around marriage, family, and commitment. Amna creates a space where both partners feel equally heard — without blame, favoritism, or judgment.

Common topics in couples therapy:

Communication breakdowns and conflict cycles. Navigating in-law relationships. Parenting disagreements. Intimacy and emotional distance. Cultural or religious differences within the marriage. Processing infidelity or broken trust. Pre-marital preparation and expectations-setting.

Ideal for: Couples at any stage — engaged couples seeking pre-marital counseling, newlyweds building strong foundations, and long-married couples working through old patterns or new challenges.

Service 03

Family
Therapy

Family therapy brings together family members to improve communication, resolve conflict, and support one another through difficulty. Whether you're dealing with a child's mental health challenges, a family member's substance use, recurring conflict between parents and teens, grief, or the stress of major life transitions, family therapy creates a structured, neutral space to do the hard work together.

Amna is particularly experienced with the dynamics of Muslim families — multigenerational households, the weight of cultural expectation, the challenge of children straddling two worlds, and the complex role of religion in family decision-making. She works with the family you have, not the family a textbook assumes you are.

Ideal for: Parents and children experiencing conflict. Blended or multigenerational families. Families supporting a member through mental health challenges. Families navigating shared grief, divorce, or relocation.

Service 04

Online
Therapy (Telehealth)

All of Amna's services are available via secure telehealth to clients anywhere in Georgia. Online sessions take place over a HIPAA-compliant video platform — the same confidentiality, the same quality of care, and the same therapeutic relationship as in-person sessions.

Research consistently shows that telehealth therapy is equally effective to in-person care for most clinical concerns, including anxiety, depression, relationship issues, and life transitions. For busy professionals, parents, college students, those without easy transportation, or anyone who feels more comfortable in their own space — online therapy is a genuinely powerful option.

How it works:

After scheduling, you'll receive a secure link to your private video session. All you need is a quiet, private space and a phone or computer. Sessions run the same length as in-person appointments and follow the same clinical approach — there's no difference in the level of care you receive.

Ideal for: Anyone in Georgia who prefers online access — parents at home, professionals with limited time, those in rural areas, or clients who find the comfort of home supports their therapeutic work.

Service 05

Trauma-Focused
Therapy

Trauma is not always visible — and it doesn't have to be dramatic to be real. Amna specializes in helping clients process unresolved traumatic experiences using trauma-informed, evidence-based methods, paced entirely to the client's readiness and comfort.

This includes childhood trauma, religious trauma, racial and identity-based trauma, immigration and acculturation trauma, domestic trauma, acute incidents, and complex PTSD. Amna is particularly experienced with trauma that is entangled with faith — including experiences of spiritual abuse, religious coercion, or a loss of faith following trauma — and creates a non-judgmental, culturally attuned space to work through it.

What trauma-focused therapy involves:

Building safety and stability first — we never dive into traumatic material before you're ready. Gradually developing the tools and capacity to process difficult memories. Addressing the ways past trauma is affecting your present-day relationships, self-image, and daily life. Moving toward integration, meaning-making, and post-traumatic growth.

Ideal for: Adults experiencing PTSD symptoms, complex trauma, religious or identity trauma, racial trauma, or any unresolved distressing experience affecting their present functioning.

Service 06

Adolescent
Therapy

Growing up Muslim in America brings a unique set of pressures — navigating two cultural worlds simultaneously, managing family expectations, experiencing Islamophobia, forming an identity under social media scrutiny, and trying to understand how faith fits into a complex modern life. Amna works with Muslim teens (ages 14 and up) with deep understanding of these specific pressures and genuine respect for where each teen is on their journey.

She works collaboratively with parents to ensure they feel informed and supported — while maintaining appropriate confidentiality with the teen and centering the teen's autonomy, voice, and dignity throughout the process. Therapy is teen-led, not parent-driven.

Common topics for teen clients:

Anxiety, depression, and mood. Identity questions and faith struggles. School stress and academic pressure. Social isolation or peer conflict. Family conflict and communication breakdowns. Social media and body image. College transition anxiety.

Ideal for: Muslim teens ages 14–17 experiencing emotional, behavioral, or identity challenges. Parents who want collaborative support — not to override their teen, but to better understand and connect with them.

Service 07

Grief &
Loss Counseling

Grief is not a problem to solve — it's a human experience to be accompanied through. Whether you are grieving the death of someone you loved, the end of a marriage, a pregnancy lost, a friendship that fell apart, a home left behind, a career or identity that no longer exists, or a version of yourself or your faith that you've had to let go — your loss is real and your grief deserves space.

Amna draws on Islamic perspectives on loss and remembrance when clients find this helpful — understanding grief through the lens of faith, trusting in God's plan, honoring the deceased through du'a, and finding meaning after loss. She also works with clients in a secular framework when that is more fitting, meeting each person where they are.

Ideal for: Anyone experiencing any form of loss — death, divorce, pregnancy, career, identity, faith, relationships, or the grief of leaving one life behind and entering another.
